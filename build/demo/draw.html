Lisp feels a bit back-to-front and inside-out when you first start! Our Rocket is the last item to be processed, and our background variable the first. How To Design Programs is a great place to learn programming fundamentals.

(require 2htdp/image)

; Here's the constants

(define WIDTH 180)
(define HEIGHT 180)
(define MIDDLE (/ WIDTH 2))

(define BACKGROUND
  (rectangle WIDTH HEIGHT "solid" "black"))

(define ROCKET
  (bitmap/file "rocket.png"))

(define MOON
  (circle 40 "solid" "white"))

; Let's make our image!

(place-image
  ROCKET MIDDLE MIDDLE
    (place-image
      MOON 10 10
        BACKGROUND))

Racket (Lisp) processes functions from the inside → out. Lisp calculates the inner-most function (data or variable) FIRST. It then works inside-out to render our ROCKET. That’s very maths-like. Try the first two chapters of HTDP, then try Elm Lang!
